The numbers underneath are aggressive. The strikes ran from 22:00 GMT Tuesday to just before 01:00 GMT Wednesday, with Iranian state media reporting explosions on Qeshm Island and in the ports of Sirik, Jask, and .

Iran retaliated early Wednesday with missiles and drones at US bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan, including the air base, according to Iranian state media. The three governments said their air defenses intercepted the missiles, with Jordan reporting five shot down and no casualties from falling shrapnel. Kuwait's foreign ministry said Wednesday that strikes hitting Kuwait International Airport and other facilities had killed at least one person and injured at least 63, per Fox News. The exchange, the first time since the April ceasefire that has hit targets across mainland Iran in waves, threatens to collapse the peace talks both sides claimed to want as recently as Monday.

Why it mattersTwenty-four hours ago both capitals were calling a conditional halt; now the US is striking Iran directly and Gulf allies are absorbing the answer. This is the closest Washington and Tehran have come to open war, and every desk from oil to Treasuries reprices on it.

Federal judge voids Trump's $100,000 H-1B fee as a tax only Congress can levy

US District Judge of the District of Massachusetts struck down the administration's $100,000 supplemental payment on new visas, ruling that "the substance and application of the $100,000 payment reveal that it is a tax" and that Congress never delegated that power to the executive. The decision, siding with 20 states, found the policy exceeded executive authority and violated the , and it swept broadly: Sorokin invalidated the agency memoranda, guidance documents, website instructions, FAQs, and fee schedules that built the program.

The fee had bitten less than advertised but bitten hard where it landed: had logged 85 payments worth $8.5 million as of mid-February, and immigration lawyers say employers who paid may now seek refunds. The administration said it will appeal. The ruling took effect immediately, reopening the fall H-1B pipeline for employers who had shelved petitions rather than pay.

Why it mattersThe tax-versus-fee holding is the interesting law: it is the same separation-of-powers logic that felled the IEEPA tariffs in February, and it gives every regulated industry a template for attacking monetized executive policy. BigLaw immigration and employment desks just got their busiest week of the year.

House sends $70 billion ICE and Border Patrol package to Trump's desk, 214-212

The House on Tuesday passed the , a roughly $70 billion package funding ICE and the Border Patrol through the end of Trump's term, by a vote of 214-212. , the California independent who caucuses with Republicans, joined every Democrat in voting no. The bill cleared the Senate last week and now goes to the president, whose signature is expected; it ends a months-long fight that exposed real cracks in the GOP conference over locking in funding past the next Congress.

The mechanics are the point: by pushing enforcement money through -style appropriations that run to January 2029, Republicans insulated Trump's immigration agenda from any future appropriations standoff, and, Democrats argue, from meaningful congressional oversight. Appropriations Democrats led by Rosa DeLauro framed the package as $70 billion for enforcement while domestic programs absorb cuts; Speaker counted it a signature win.

Why it mattersFunding an agency through the end of a presidency is a structural move, not a budget line: it converts an annual oversight lever into a one-time vote. The 214-212 margin says even that barely holds.

The 48-team World Cup kicks off tomorrow at the Azteca, with a record $850 million ad machine behind it

The 2026 FIFA World Cup opens Thursday with Mexico against South Africa at the , the start of a 104-match tournament across the US, Mexico, and Canada that runs to July 19. The field expands from 32 teams to 48, with Uzbekistan, Jordan, Cape Verde, and Curacao all debuting; the US opens against Paraguay on Friday night. In the US, Fox, FS1, Telemundo, and Universo carry all 104 matches, and Fox is streaming the opener and the US match free on with a sign-in.

The business is the record: Fox and Telemundo together are projected to take in as much as $850 million in ad revenue, against $384.3 million the last time the tournament ran in its normal summer window in 2018. The expansion gives Fox roughly 63 percent more than the time-shifted 2022 Qatar edition, and home-soil staging puts every match in sellable hours. FIFA, for its part, projects the most lucrative cycle in its history, on the order of $13 billion.

Why it mattersThis is the biggest live-rights economics experiment ever run in the US market, and the revenue mechanics, expansion-as-inventory, streaming funnels, host-market ad rates, are exactly the deal architecture an EASL practice lives on for the next decade.
