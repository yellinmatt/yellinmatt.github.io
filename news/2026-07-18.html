America bombs the bridges of Bandar Abbas, and the war for the Strait of Hormuz reaches its seventh night

United States forces struck at least seven bridges in southern Iran overnight, moving to cut the port city of Bandar AbbasPlaceIran’s largest commercial port, on the Strait of Hormuz, and home to the naval headquarters of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps; the strikes aim to sever its road and rail links inland. off from the highways and rail lines running toward Shiraz and Tehran. At least eight people were killed and 20 injured in the overnight attacks on bridges in Hormozgan province, according to Iran’s state news agency; a junction on the Bandar Abbas rail line and Iranshahr Airport farther east were also hit. It was the seventh consecutive night of an intensifying American campaign, after President Trump threatened the bridges in a Fox News interview earlier in the week.

The stated aim remains Iran’s grip on the Strait of HormuzPlaceThe narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which roughly a fifth of the world’s seaborne oil moved before the war; traffic has since fallen to about a dozen ships a week., where daily traffic has dwindled to roughly a dozen ships. The fight is spilling into the Gulf: the US also struck a water desalination plant in Kuwait, Jordan’s air defenses intercepted 10 Iranian missiles early Saturday, and Kuwait, Bahrain and Saudi Arabia came under fire or issued shelter warnings. Choking a major port and pushing the war into Gulf capitals widens a conflict that began as a blockade standoff over a single strait. With Hormuz already throttled and Iran having told the Houthis this week to ready the Bab el-Mandeb, both of the Middle East’s principal export routes are now on the board at once. The oil-risk premium has stopped being a story about one waterway, and the distance between an Iranian warning and a genuine energy shock is narrowing with each night of strikes.

Why it mattersA war that started over one strait is now cutting ports and drawing in Gulf states, and the gap between a contained campaign and a global oil shock is exactly the risk a capital-markets desk or an energy client cannot look away from.

Framing noteThe right (Washington Times, Fox) frames the bridge strikes as an expanding campaign tightening the screws on Tehran; the center-left (CNN) leads on a conflict “showing no sign of de-escalating” and the mounting civilian and regional toll.

Brussels orders Google to open Android and Search to its AI rivals

The European CommissionInstitutionThe European Union’s executive arm and its competition enforcer, which polices the bloc’s digital-market rules and can fine or bind the largest platforms. adopted two binding measures Thursday requiring Google to give rival AI assistants the same reach into Android that its own Gemini enjoys: the camera, the microphone, whatever is on screen, a wake word that fires with the display off, and the ability to drive other apps in the background by imitating taps and typing. The access spans 11 Android feature groups, subject to certification and user consent, and must ship in the next major release, Android 18, no later than August 1, 2027. The orders come under the Digital Markets ActConceptThe EU’s 2022 law imposing conduct rules on designated “gatekeeper” platforms, meant to force interoperability and curb self-preferencing, backed by fines up to 10% of global revenue..

A second measure forces Google to share anonymized search ranking, query, click and view data with qualifying recipients on FRANDConceptFair, reasonable and non-discriminatory terms, a licensing standard borrowed from patent law that bars a dominant holder from favoring itself or gouging rivals for essential access. terms starting in January 2027. The order is widely read as a lever for Anthropic, OpenAI and France’s MistralInstitutionThe Paris-based AI lab positioned as Europe’s open-model champion, a frequent beneficiary of EU efforts to loosen the grip of American platforms., whose assistants could soon operate an Android phone directly rather than through Google’s.

Why it mattersIf the assistant that controls the phone no longer has to be Google’s, the most valuable distribution channel in consumer AI is pried open by regulation rather than by competition, a structural shift a technology-and-markets lawyer should be tracking.
