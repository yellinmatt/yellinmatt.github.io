Russia hits Kyiv with its largest barrage of the war, including a nuclear-capable Oreshnik

Overnight into Sunday, Russia launched about 90 missiles and roughly 600 drones at Kyiv and the surrounding region, an assault Ukraine's air force called one of the largest of the war. At least four people were killed and more than 60 wounded, and about 30 residential buildings in the capital were damaged or destroyed. The attack ran for hours and, Ukrainian officials said, left damage in every district of the city.

Among the weapons was the Oreshnik, an experimental hypersonic intermediate-range ballistic missile capable of carrying a nuclear warhead, fired for only the third time in the full-scale war. It hit Bila Tserkva, a city about 50 miles south of Kyiv. Russia loosed the barrage hours after Ukraine mounted its own drone strikes inside Russian territory, part of a sharpening tit-for-tat as ceasefire diplomacy stalls and Washington's attention stays fixed on the Middle East.

President Volodymyr Zelensky said the strikes hit civilian and cultural sites, including the Museum of Chornobyl, and pressed Western capitals for tougher sanctions and more air defense. The U.S. embassy in Kyiv had warned of a possible major strike in the preceding 24 hours.

"They're really insane. It's important that this doesn't go unpunished."
— President Volodymyr Zelensky, May 24

Why it mattersThe strike shows how far ceasefire talk has drifted from the battlefield even as Washington brokers a Middle East deal. A widening war keeps an energy-risk premium, and the inflation it feeds, firmly in the picture for rate-watchers.

'The Mandalorian and Grogu' tops the holiday box office, yet marks Disney's weakest Star Wars launch

Disney and Lucasfilm's "The Mandalorian and Grogu" led the Memorial Day weekend with roughly $82 million across three days and an estimated $102 million through Monday, plus about $64 million abroad for a global start near $145 million to $165 million. Exit polls handed it an "A-" CinemaScore. It is the first theatrical outing for the pair who built a following on Disney+.

The figure is also the lowest domestic opening for any Star Wars film since Disney bought Lucasfilm in 2012, undercutting 2018's "Solo." The studio's hedge was cost discipline: a lean production budget reported around $165 million, well below recent franchise tentpoles, which lowers the bar to profit and reflects a post-streaming caution about how much theatrical Star Wars can still command.

Why it mattersFor anyone tracking entertainment economics, this is the franchise question in miniature: strong scores, soft turnout, and a studio betting that a cheaper film plus goodwill beats chasing a record opening.

Turkish police storm the main opposition's headquarters, deepening a political crisis

On Sunday, Turkish police stormed the offices of the Republican People's Party (CHP), the country's main opposition, firing tear gas and rubber bullets at supporters and officials who had barricaded inside for three days. The raid followed an appeals-court ruling that nullified the November 2023 election of party chairman Ozgur Ozel and ordered him replaced by his predecessor, Kemal Kilicdaroglu, who led the party for 13 years without winning a national vote.

The CHP, which dealt President Erdogan's AKP a heavy defeat in the 2024 municipal elections, has vowed to resist, calling the corruption-tinged legal cases an effort to neutralize it before the next national contest. Correspondents inside the building described doors and furniture destroyed as officers cleared the leadership out.

Why it mattersTurkey is a NATO member and a pivotal emerging-market economy; an opposition crackdown lifts the political-risk premium for investors and tightens the West's bind over a strategically essential, increasingly authoritarian ally.

Secret Service kills a gunman who opened fire near the White House, wounding a bystander

Secret Service officers shot and killed a man who pulled a weapon from his bag and opened fire near a security checkpoint at 17th Street and Pennsylvania Avenue around 6 p.m. Saturday, the agency said. A bystander was struck and remained in critical condition; no officers were hurt. President Trump was at the White House at the time and was not affected.

Officials identified the suspect as 21-year-old Nasire Best, who had a prior 2025 run-in with the Secret Service after trying to enter the White House and was hospitalized for mental-health treatment. It was the third instance of gunfire in Trump's vicinity in the past month, reviving scrutiny of the protective detail.

Why it mattersA pattern of armed incidents near the president is a national-security story in its own right and another test of a Secret Service still rebuilding trust after a year of high-profile lapses.

China's deadliest mine blast in years kills at least 82; executives detained as investigators probe safety lapses

A gas explosion late Friday at the privately run Liushenyu coal mine in Shanxi province killed at least 82 workers, China's deadliest coal-mine disaster in years, with 247 miners underground at the time. The toll, briefly reported as high as 90, was revised down amid what officials called chaotic conditions and inaccurate information from the operator; two miners remained missing as of Sunday, and more than 120 were injured.

A State Council investigation team promised a "rigorous and uncompromising" probe, and executives of the Shanxi Tongzhou group that runs the mine were detained. State broadcaster CCTV said the mine's blueprints did not match its actual layout, hampering rescuers, and the site had appeared on a 2024 national list of disaster-prone mines.

Why it mattersThe disaster is a reminder that China's industrial base still runs on coal, and that the gap between safety rules on paper and enforcement underground remains lethal.

China launches Shenzhou 23, sending up its first Hong Kong astronaut and starting its first yearlong mission

China launched the Shenzhou 23 spacecraft from the Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center on Sunday night, carrying commander Zhu Yangzhu, Zhang Zhiyuan, and Lai Ka-ying, the first astronaut from Hong Kong to reach orbit. The crew docked with the Tiangong station to begin a six-month stay and an in-orbit handover with the Shenzhou 21 astronauts, who have been aloft more than 200 days.

One of the three will remain aboard for a full year, a first for China's program, to study human adaptability in long-duration spaceflight; officials have not said which. A Pakistani astronaut is slated to arrive on Shenzhou 24. The steady cadence underscores how routine, and how geopolitically pointed, China's crewed program has become.

Why it mattersA maturing, independent Chinese station plus a yearlong endurance mission is a marker in the U.S.-China space competition, landing as NASA's own crewed plans face budget pressure.
