America turns 250, and the birthday belongs to Trump

The United States marked the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence on Saturday with the largest fireworks display in the country's history over the National MallPlaceThe two-mile landscaped expanse between the Capitol and the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, the traditional stage for the capital's Fourth of July fireworks. and a primetime address from President Trump that ran closer to a campaign rally than a civic benediction. Storms forced nearly two hours of evacuations; Trump took the stage around 11:15 p.m., flanked by an original 1777 flag and the flag that draped Abraham Lincoln's coffin, with Medal of Honor recipients brought up to salute. The semiquincentennialConceptThe 250th anniversary of the 1776 Declaration of Independence. The federal Freedom 250 program built a year of events around July 4, 2026. anchored a sprawling program: celebrations in Boston, Philadelphia, New York and Charleston, the Great American State Fair, Sail250, a UFC card on the South Lawn, a Grand Prix around the Mall, and World Cup matches in US host cities on the date itself.

The speech mixed patriotism with grievance. Trump called the country "the crowning achievement of human history" and "a nation of winners," praised his war in Iran, railed against "communists," and pushed his plan to restrict mail-in voting. The historian Jon MeachamPersonThe Pulitzer-winning presidential historian and biographer. He said Trump had "hijacked" the 250th anniversary. said Trump had "hijacked" the anniversary; the Public Integrity Project sued over the UFC's use of the South Lawn. A day earlier the president had disclosed personal income topping $2 billion, then flew to North Dakota aboard a new Air Force One gifted by Qatar.

The mood in the country was harder to read than the pyrotechnics. A Reuters/IpsosInstitutionThe wire service's recurring national opinion poll conducted with Ipsos. It found one in five Americans would not celebrate Independence Day this year. poll found one in five Americans, including a quarter of Democrats and 8% of Republicans, would not celebrate at all, and nearly 70% said they were dissatisfied with the country's direction. A Marquette Law School survey in June found only about a quarter had heard "a lot" about the 250th. The anniversary that was meant to unite arrived instead as a measure of how divided the union has become.

"Freedom's triumph over tyranny, liberty's conquest over oppression, and the enduring victory of the American spirit."
— President Trump, National Mall, July 4

Why it mattersThe 250th is a once-in-history civic marker, and its capture by partisan politics is itself the story: the anniversary meant to transcend the moment instead mirrored it.

Framing noteThe Left (CNN, NPR) cast the night as a campaign-style address that "hijacked" a unifying ritual; the Right (Fox News) led with a president touting "a nation of winners"; the wire poll (Reuters/Ipsos) foregrounded a country too divided to celebrate as one.

The Front · Markets · Washington

A 57,000-job miss cracks the soft-landing story

The economy added just 57,000 jobs in June, the Bureau of Labor StatisticsInstitutionThe Labor Department's independent statistical agency that produces the monthly jobs and inflation reports. reported Thursday, with the release pulled forward a day for the holiday. That was less than half the roughly 115,000 economists expected and the weakest month in four, and revisions cut 74,000 from April and May. Unemployment ticked down to 4.2% from 4.3%, but for the wrong reason: labor-force participationConceptThe share of working-age adults employed or actively looking for work. A falling rate can flatter the jobless number by shrinking the pool being counted. fell 0.3 point to 61.5%, its lowest since March 2021, as 507,000 people left the household count. Leisure and hospitality shed 61,000.

Markets read it as a growth scare that still would not force the Fed's hand. Two-year Treasury yields fell 3.5 basis pointsConceptOne hundredth of a percentage point, the unit bond traders use to measure moves in yields. to 4.13%, and traders trimmed the odds of a September cut to roughly 51% from 63%. New Fed chair Kevin WarshPersonThe new Federal Reserve chair and a former Fed governor, sworn in this spring. He is holding the policy rate at 3.50 to 3.75%., holding rates at 3.50 to 3.75% with inflation at a three-year high, called the labor market "steady." The print landed the same week US stocks closed their best quarter since 2020.

Why it mattersA cooling labor market plus sticky inflation is the stagflation-lite bind that will govern the rate path a capital-markets desk prices all year; a weak number that does not unlock a cut is the worst of both worlds for issuance windows.

The Court frees the president to fire independent regulators

In Trump v. Slaughter, the Supreme Court on June 29 overruled Humphrey's ExecutorConceptThe 1935 ruling that upheld for-cause removal protection for independent-agency heads. Its reversal strips the legal shield the SEC, FTC, and FCC leaderships relied on., the 1935 precedent that let Congress shield the heads of independent agencies from at-will presidential removal. The 6-3 majority, written by Chief Justice John RobertsPersonThe Chief Justice of the United States, who wrote the majority opinion freeing the president to remove agency heads without cause., held that the president may remove an FTCInstitutionThe Federal Trade Commission, the five-member antitrust and consumer-protection agency at the center of Trump v. Slaughter. commissioner without cause, and the reasoning reaches the multimember commissions that run much of the administrative state. In a companion case, Trump v. Cook, a 5-4 Court let Fed Governor Lisa CookPersonThe Federal Reserve governor Trump moved to remove; the Court let her keep her seat, carving the Fed out of the new rule. keep her seat, carving the Federal Reserve out of the new rule.

The immediate target was the FTC, but the doctrine's shadow falls on the SEC, the CFTC, the FCC, and the NLRB, whose bipartisan-commission structure has rested on Humphrey's for 90 years. Markets care because an SEC chair removable at will is a more political SEC: enforcement priorities, rulemaking, and the pace of registered offerings all become more responsive to the White House. The Fed carve-out, justified by its distinct role over monetary policy, is the tell that the majority understood exactly how much it was moving.

Why it mattersIf the agency that polices the capital markets can be reshaped by a single election, the regulatory backdrop to every IPO and enforcement action becomes a political variable rather than a fixed one, which is precisely the world a capital-markets lawyer has to advise into.

Framing noteThe Right (Fox News, National Review) framed it as restoring accountability over an unelected "fourth branch"; the Left (NYT, NPR) framed it as a landmark expansion of presidential power that guts agency independence.

Microsoft bets $2.5 billion that AI's next act is deployment, not models

Microsoft on July 2 launched the Frontier Company, a $2.5 billion unit staffing 6,000 engineers embedded inside customers to build and run AI systems on the ground. Launch partners include the London Stock Exchange GroupInstitutionThe London-based exchange and financial-data company, owner of Refinitiv, and a launch partner for Microsoft's embedded-AI unit., Unilever, Land O'Lakes, and Accenture. Clients keep the intellectual property and outputs, and their data is not used to train Microsoft's models. The commercial chief Judson AlthoffPersonMicrosoft's chief commercial officer, who leads its enterprise sales organization and now the Frontier Company. said the effort "goes beyond forward-deployed engineering."

The move more than doubles the $1 billion forward-deployed engineeringConceptThe Palantir-pioneered model of embedding a vendor's engineers inside a customer to build systems in place, rather than selling software off the shelf. push Amazon unveiled the same week, and it signals where the value is migrating. With frontier-model gains narrowing and open-weightConceptAn AI model whose parameters are downloadable and self-hostable, letting rivals undercut closed labs on price, as China's GLM-5.2 did this week. rivals undercutting on price, the labs and their patrons are pivoting from selling access to selling deployment: the human-plus-model integration work that actually changes how a company runs. That is the same layer where legal, compliance, and back-office functions get rebuilt.

Why it mattersThe deployment layer, not the model, is where AI reorganizes white-collar work, law included; a $2.5 billion, 6,000-engineer bet on embedding is the clearest signal yet that the money has moved from capability to integration.

The Front · Corporate · Philadelphia

Comcast splits itself in two, cleaving media from connectivity

Comcast said June 30 it will separate into two public companies: a media-and-entertainment company holding NBCUniversalInstitutionComcast's media arm: the NBC networks, Universal Pictures, and theme parks, now bound for its own public company with Sky. and Sky, and a connectivity company built around Xfinity broadband. The breakup, expected to take about a year, follows last year's spin of Comcast's cable networks and leaves CEO Brian RobertsPersonComcast's longtime chairman and CEO, who will oversee the separation but run neither resulting company directly. running neither operating company directly. Charter shares jumped roughly 10% on speculation the reshuffle presages further consolidation.

The split is the latest admission that the cable pipe and the studio no longer belong under one roof: connectivity is a stable, capital-intensive utility, while media is a content arms race with a different cost of capital and a different set of buyers. It also frees each half to be a cleaner acquisition currency, arriving as the Paramount-Warner combination and other megadeals redraw the industry. For an entertainment lawyer, the structural separation of distribution from content is the decade's defining media question.

Why it mattersEvery distributor is being re-underwritten as either a utility or a content bet, and the legal work of unbundling them, the spins, carve-outsConceptA transaction that separates a business unit into a standalone entity, often by spinoff or IPO, a staple of capital-markets practice., and antitrust reviews, is exactly the capital-markets-meets-entertainment work Matthew is aiming at.

Iran buries Khamenei before millions as the war pauses

Iran entered the second day of a six-day state funeral on Sunday for Ayatollah Ali KhameneiPersonIran's supreme leader from 1989 until his death in February, the country's ultimate political and religious authority., the supreme leaderConceptIran's highest office, holding final say over the military, judiciary, and foreign policy, above the elected president. of 37 years who was killed on February 28 in the opening US-Israeli strikes of this year's war. Officials expect 15 to 20 million mourners, which would make it the largest state funeral in the country's history. Sunday was given to funeral prayers for Khamenei and four family members killed in the same strike; the main Tehran procession runs Monday, prayers follow in Qom, and burial is set for July 9 at the Imam Reza shrine in MashhadPlaceIran's second city and holiest site, home to the Imam Reza shrine, where Khamenei, born there, will be buried July 9..

Three of Khamenei's sons joined the mourning, though not MojtabaPersonThe late leader's second son, long floated as a possible successor; his absence from the public mourning was conspicuous., long seen as a potential successor, whose absence drew notice. Artificial Intelligence

Fable 5 comes back online. Commerce lifted the June 12 export controls on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on July 1, restoring Fable 5 globally across Claude products after Amazon researchers had bypassed its guardrails to surface exploit code; a new classifier now blocks the technique more than 99% of the time, and Anthropic conceded shared jailbreak-severity standards and pre-release government access. · Anthropic

China's open weights undercut the labs. Z.ai's GLM-5.2, released open-weight under an MIT license the week of July 1, climbed developer leaderboards at roughly one-sixth the cost of closed frontier models, beating GPT-5.5 on several long-horizon coding benchmarks and squeezing OpenAI and Anthropic margins as both prepare to list. · VentureBeat

The web starts charging AI crawlers. Cloudflare rolled out purpose-based bot controls to all customers on July 1-2, sorting AI bots into Search, Agent, and Training; from September 15 new domains block Agent and Training crawlers by default on ad-monetized pages, and Cloudflare opened direct pay-per-crawl billing, a content-licensing lever with real intellectual-property weight. · Cloudflare
