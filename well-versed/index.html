The Goldberg Variations

Bach BWV 988, published 1741 by Balthasar Schmid of Nuremberg, an Aria plus 30 variations plus the Aria da capo built on the bass line, every third variation a canon at a widening interval, Quodlibet at variation 30. Forkel's 1802 Keyserlingk legend, now mostly rejected. The 14 canons (BWV 1087) found in Bach's Handexemplar in 1974. Glenn Gould's 1955 debut recording at Columbia 30th Street Studio (38:34) and the 1981 digital re-reading in the same room (51:18) bookending his career.

2026-05-18
Ozymandias

Shelley's 1817 Christmas-competition sonnet with Horace Smith, published in The Examiner 11 January 1818 under the pen name Glirastes, written before the Younger Memnon bust of Ramesses II reached London. Diodorus Siculus as source, the four-narrator Russian-doll frame, the hybrid ABABACDCEDEFEF rhyme scheme as form-as-ruin, Belzoni's extraction from the Ramesseum, the bust now in British Museum Room 4.
