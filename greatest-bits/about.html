About / The Project

The Project

A hand-built home for the art of stand-up — and an argument about what's great.

Non-commercialFan-madeAlways a work in progress

Greatest Bits catalogues stand-up comedy in one place: an index of every comic, every special, and every bit, three ranked lists of the hundred best of each, and a long-form history of the form told through its bits. It is built and ranked slowly, in public, by one person, for the love of the art rather than as a business.

The goal is simple and a little stubborn: a beautiful, accurate, browsable record of the funniest people who ever held a mic, one that always sends you back to the artists and their official work.

What counts as a bit

The bit is the atom: a single routine, joke, story, or sustained run that stands on its own. We cast wide on type, because greatness shows up in every shape.

Built routinesLong-form storiesOne-linersCrowd workCallbacksAct-outs
Carlin's "Seven Words." Pryor's heart-attack act-out. A tight Hedberg one-liner run.

Different shapes, same unit: the piece you'd quote to explain why someone is great. Two things support every bit and link back to it — the comic who performs it, and the special, album, set, or broadcast it comes from. The model is built to stretch, so new kinds of entries can be added later without a rebuild.

How it's built
Principle 01

Discovery is systematic

The catalogue is filled broadly first, with a source for every entry, so judgment always works on a complete board, never a blank page. Nothing important is left out just because it's obscure; thinly documented bits are logged with the gap flagged, not dropped.

Principle 02

Ranking is taste-driven

The lists are ordered by hand, one placement at a time. There is no algorithm and no crowd poll behind the numbers: this is a personal canon, argued and owned. The reasoning is shown; the verdict is a point of view, not a measurement.

The list is alive

Comics and bits are added as they're watched and reconsidered, and everything stays re-rankable as the canon grows. It's a record kept in motion, not a monument poured once.

Rights & respect for the work
§

Greatest Bits indexes stand-up; it never passes the work off as its own. Nothing is hosted here. Photographs identify and credit each artist, clips embed from official channels or link straight to the special, and everything points you back to the source.

If something is wrong, miscredited, or you'd like it removed, get in touch and it comes down, no questions asked. The full sourcing rules and the ranking method live on the Method & Sources page.
