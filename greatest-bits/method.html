Method &
Sources

How the catalogue is built, how the ranking works, and where every fact and image comes from.

Sources citedNothing fabricatedAlways re-rankable

Two ideas run the project, and one rule sits above both. Discovery is systematic and ranking is taste-driven — and nothing here is ever invented. Where a fact, a link, or a clip can't be verified, the gap is shown plainly instead of papered over.

Discovery

The catalogue is filled broadly first, so judgment works on a complete board, never a blank page. Every comic, special, and bit is logged with a source. Bits that matter but are thinly documented are kept and flagged, not dropped, because a record that quietly omits the hard-to-source cases isn't a complete record.

How the ranking works

The lists are ordered by hand. A bit lands in a tier first, then takes a fine position by head-to-head comparison against the bits already in that tier.

Pantheon
The defining work. Changed what the form could do.
Classic
Enduring, widely revered, quoted for decades.
Great
Excellent and distinctive, just below the canon's peak.
Notable
Worth knowing, on the board, in the conversation.

An advisory scorecard informs the eye but never decides the order. Five axes get weighed, then set aside for judgment:

1  Craft2  Originality3  Influence4  Cultural impact5  Longevity

The score is a point of view, not a measurement. This is one editor's canon, argued and owned — not a weighted vote of hundreds of critics, the way the big magazine and institute lists are built. That makes it openly subjective, and the trade is transparency: the reasoning is shown, and everything stays re-rankable as the canon grows.

The numbers you see now are provisional, flagged as illustrative until the real verdicts land.

Sources & verification

Official, or flagged

Nothing is hosted on this site. Every clip, link, and credit is checked before it ships; a dead or re-uploaded link is worse than an honest "coming soon." This is the protocol, run on every entry.

  1. Find, then verify

    Candidates are found by research, then each one is checked individually. A summary is an intention; only a passing check ships.

  2. Clips must be official and embeddable

    A clip is embedded inline only if it is embeddable and sits on an official channel — the comic's or estate's own, or a network or label (Netflix Is A Joke, Comedy Central, HBO, Just for Laughs, and the like). Re-uploads are never embedded.

  3. Links are checked live

    Every website and channel link is requested and must resolve before it ships. Dead links are dropped and shown as a coming soon badge rather than guessed at.

  4. Embed official, link out otherwise

    Where no official clip exists, the page links out to a long-standing upload rather than leave a gap. Nothing is ever re-hosted or re-uploaded here.

Imagery & credits

Images are used to identify each work and point you to it, sized for recognition and always credited. Comic photographs come from Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA); special and album posters come from TMDB. Clips embed from official YouTube and Spotify, or link straight to the special. Credit lines run in the footer of every page.

What we never do
✗
  • ✗Never fabricate a link, quote, date, or attribution. If it can't be verified, the gap is shown.
  • ✗Never publish a full transcript of a copyrighted bit — quoted lines stay short, as commentary, and attributed.
  • ✗Never self-host or re-upload a clip. Everything plays from, or links to, the official source.
Corrections & takedowns

Spotted a mistake, a mis-credit, or something you want removed? It will be fixed or taken down promptly, no questions asked. Get in touch. All rights remain with their owners, and everything here links back to the official source.
